Travel Information

Essential information for planning your trip to Namibia

Visa & Entry Requirements

Tourist Visa

Most visitors can obtain a 90-day tourist visa on arrival. Required documents:

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Proof of sufficient funds
  • Yellow fever certificate if applicable

Visa-Free Countries

Citizens of the following countries don't need a visa for stays up to 90 days:

  • United States
  • United Kingdom
  • European Union countries
  • Canada
  • Australia

Customs Regulations

Important customs information:

  • Duty-free allowance: 400 cigarettes, 2L wine, 1L spirits
  • Firearms require special permit
  • Strict controls on wildlife products

Transportation in Namibia

Car Rental

4x4 vehicles recommended for exploring remote areas. Major rental companies available at airports.

  • Drive on the left side
  • International driver's license required
  • Comprehensive insurance recommended

Domestic Flights

Efficient way to cover long distances between major destinations.

  • Air Namibia domestic routes
  • Small charter flights available
  • Book in advance during peak season

Public Transport

Limited but available between major towns.

  • Intercape Mainliner buses
  • Minibus taxis for shorter routes
  • Train services between major cities

Climate & Best Time to Visit

Seasons

Namibia has distinct dry and wet seasons:

  • Dry Season: May-October (Best for wildlife)
  • Wet Season: November-April (Lush landscapes)

Temperatures

Varies by region:

  • Coast: 15-25°C year-round
  • Desert: Extreme variations (0-40°C)
  • North: Warmer and more humid

Packing Tips

Essential items to pack:

  • Sun protection (hat, sunscreen)
  • Layered clothing
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Binoculars for wildlife viewing
